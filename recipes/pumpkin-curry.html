Pumpkin Curry

One pot anti-inflammatory pumpkin curry.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbs Ground Turmeric
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tsp Ground Fenugreek
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 5 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • Thumb-sized Piece Ginger, chopped fine
  • 1 tsp Chilli Powder
  • Pinch Sea Salt
  • 250g Pumpkin or Sweet Potato, peeled & cubed
  • 1 can Black Beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 Head Broccoli, cut into florets
  • 4 Dried Curry Leaves, optional
  • 1 litre Vegetable Stock
  • 1 Cinnamon Stick
  • Big Handful Fresh Greens, like kale, spinach, chard. Chopped small.
  • 1/2 cup/125ml Coconut Milk
  • 2 tsp Ground Garam Masala

Serve With

  • Wild or Brown Rice, I stir through lemon & coriander
  • Toasted Flaked Almonds

Method

  1. Toast the turmeric, mustard seeds, cumin & fenugreek for 2 minutes in a dry pan over a low heat. Remove them from the pan then sauté the onion, garlic & ginger. Add the spices back to the pan along with the chilli & cook for 5 minutes – stirring often.
  2. Add the pumpkin, broccoli, black beans & cook for a couple more minutes before adding the curry leaves, vegetable stock & cinnamon stick. Pop a lid on the pan and let the curry bubble away for 15 minutes.
  3. To serve stir through your greens, coconut milk & garam masala. Let the mix bubble away for a few minutes then serve up with rice & toasted almonds.
